Discover New York City

Explore the vibrant and diverse tapestry of New York City, where every borough has its own unique charm. From iconic skyscrapers to world-class museums, NYC offers endless experiences!

62 million visitors/year

New York City is one of the most visited cities in the world, known for its cultural diversity and iconic landmarks.

100+ Universities

NYC is home to many prestigious universities and colleges, making it a hub for academic excellence.

Landmarks

Discover the Statue of Liberty, Times Square, Central Park, and more.

Top Landmarks to Visit

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, a gift from France, stands on Liberty Island as a symbol of freedom and democracy. Visitors can take a ferry to the island to explore the statue and its museum. Climb up to the crown for a breathtaking view of New York Harbor. The statue's design by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and its internal framework by Gustave Eiffel make it an engineering marvel.

Times Square

Times Square, known as "The Cross-roads of the World," is a bustling commercial and entertainment hub. Famous for its neon lights, Broadway theaters, and massive electronic billboards, it’s a must-see for any visitor. Explore the vibrant street performers, dine in diverse restaurants, and catch a Broadway show. The New Year's Eve ball drop is an iconic event held here annually.

Central Park

Central Park is an expansive green oasis in the middle of Manhattan. Spanning 843 acres, it offers a serene escape from the city bustle with attractions like Bethesda Terrace, Bow Bridge, and the Central Park Zoo. Enjoy a leisurely walk, rent a bike, or take a horse-drawn carriage ride. The park also hosts various events and performances throughout the year.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building, an iconic skyscraper, offers stunning views of New York City from its observation decks on the 86th and 102nd floors. Completed in 1931, it was the tallest building in the world for nearly 40 years. The building's Art Deco design and its role in numerous films and TV shows make it a quintessential New York experience. Visit at sunset for a spectacular view of the city lights.

New York Cuisine

New York Pizza

New York-style pizza is known for its large, thin, and foldable slices. Topped with tomato sauce and mozzarella, it’s often enjoyed with a variety of additional toppings. The crispy yet chewy crust and flavorful sauce make it a local favorite. Visit famous pizzerias like Di Fara Pizza or Joe's Pizza to experience this iconic dish.

Hot Dog

New York City hot dogs are a must-try street food. Typically served with a variety of toppings such as mustard, sauerkraut, onions, and relish, these hot dogs can be found from iconic vendors like Gray’s Papaya or Nathan’s Famous. Enjoying a hot dog from a street vendor is a quintessential New York experience.

Bagel

The New York bagel is a beloved breakfast item, known for its chewy texture and variety of toppings. Often enjoyed with cream cheese and smoked salmon, bagels are a staple in the city's culinary scene. Try a classic bagel at renowned spots like Russ & Daughters or Ess-a-Bagel.

New York Traditions

Thanksgiving Day Parade

Held annually on Thanksgiving Day, the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade is a quintessential New York event. Featuring giant balloons, colorful floats, and performances by marching bands, the parade draws millions of spectators each year. It’s a festive celebration that showcases the city's spirit and creativity.

Broadway Shows

Broadway is synonymous with world-class theater and musicals. With a history of over a century, Broadway continues to offer a diverse range of performances from classic plays to contemporary musicals. Catch a show in one of the historic theaters and experience the magic of live performance in the Theater District.

New Year's Eve in Times Square

Each year on New Year’s Eve, millions gather in Times Square for the iconic ball drop, a tradition since 1907. The celebration, broadcasted live worldwide, features dazzling lights, confetti, and performances. With its vibrant atmosphere, live music, and countdown to midnight, it’s a unique way to experience New York City's energy as you welcome the New Year.
