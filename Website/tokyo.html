Discover Tokyo

Unveil the unique blend of modern and traditional culture in Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital. From its futuristic skyline to ancient temples, this city offers an experience like no other.

14 million residents

Tokyo is the most populous metropolitan area in the world.

30+ Universities

Tokyo is a hub for higher education with prestigious institutions like the University of Tokyo.

Landmarks

Discover iconic sites like Tokyo Tower, Shibuya Crossing, and more.

Top Landmarks to Visit

Tokyo Tower

Inspired by the Eiffel Tower, the Tokyo Tower is a communications and observation tower. It offers panoramic views of the city, including sights of Mount Fuji on clear days. The tower lights up beautifully at night, making it a symbol of Tokyo's blend of modernity and tradition.

Shibuya Crossing

Often referred to as the world’s busiest pedestrian crossing, Shibuya Crossing is a symbol of Tokyo’s fast-paced urban life. It's surrounded by neon billboards, shopping malls, and is a popular gathering point for locals and tourists alike.

Sensoji Temple

Located in the Asakusa district, Sensoji Temple is Tokyo’s oldest and most visited temple. The temple is dedicated to the Buddhist deity Kannon, and its entrance, marked by the grand Kaminarimon (Thunder Gate), is iconic. The vibrant market street leading to the temple offers traditional snacks and souvenirs.

Japanese Cuisine

Sushi

Perhaps Japan's most famous culinary export, sushi consists of vinegared rice paired with a variety of ingredients such as seafood, vegetables, and sometimes tropical fruits. It's a delicacy loved worldwide.

Ramen

This iconic Japanese noodle soup, made with wheat noodles, savory broth, and various toppings like meat, seaweed, and green onions, is a comfort food favorite. Each region of Japan offers its unique take on ramen.

Tempura

Tempura involves lightly battered and deep-fried seafood and vegetables. This Japanese delicacy is known for its crispiness and delicate flavor. Tokyo's tempura restaurants range from upscale dining to casual eateries, offering a wide variety of tempura dishes. For a true experience, try a tempura omakase, where the chef selects seasonal ingredients.

Japanese Traditions

Tea Ceremony

The Japanese tea ceremony, or "Chanoyu," is a ritualistic way of preparing and drinking green tea, typically in a traditional tearoom setting. This practice is deeply symbolic of Japanese culture, embodying principles of harmony, respect, purity, and tranquility.

Cherry Blossom Festival

Known as "Hanami," the Cherry Blossom Festival celebrates the blooming of cherry trees across Japan, typically in the spring. Friends and families gather beneath the blooming trees for picnics, celebrating the transient beauty of the blossoms.

Sumida River Fireworks Festival

The Sumida River Fireworks Festival, held every summer, is a major event in Tokyo. This tradition features spectacular fireworks displays along the Sumida River, attracting huge crowds. The festival offers a vibrant atmosphere with food stalls, traditional clothing, and a festive spirit, making it a great way to experience Tokyo's summer culture.
