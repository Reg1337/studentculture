Discover London

Explore the rich history and vibrant culture of London, the capital of the United Kingdom. From historic landmarks to modern attractions, London offers a blend of tradition and innovation.

9 million residents

London is a bustling metropolis known for its diverse population and cultural influence.

40+ Universities

Landmarks

Discover iconic sites like the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, and the London Eye.

Top Landmarks to Visit

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the grand residence of the British monarch and a symbol of the British monarchy. It serves as the official London residence of the Queen and hosts state occasions and royal receptions. Visitors can witness the Changing of the Guard ceremony and, during the summer months, tour the State Rooms. The palace’s rich history and role in royal traditions make it a must-visit attraction in London.

The London Eye

The London Eye, or Millennium Wheel, is a giant Ferris wheel offering panoramic views of London. Standing 135 meters tall, it has 32 capsules providing a 360-degree view. The ride takes about 30 minutes and provides a unique perspective of landmarks like the Houses of Parliament and Buckingham Palace. It's a major tourist attraction with stunning city views, day or night.

Tower of London

The Tower of London, built in 1066 by William the Conqueror, has served as a royal palace, prison, and treasury. It houses the Crown Jewels and offers tours led by Beefeaters, who share stories of its history. The White Tower is the oldest part of the complex, providing insights into medieval and royal life. It’s a captivating destination for those interested in British history.

Big Ben and the Houses of Parliament

Big Ben, officially the Elizabeth Tower, is a striking clock tower at the Palace of Westminster. Known for its Gothic architecture, it’s a defining feature of London’s skyline. The Palace of Westminster is where the UK Parliament meets, with ornate interiors and grand halls. Visitors can enjoy the area’s historic significance and view the impressive exterior of both Big Ben and the Palace.

London Cuisine

Fish and Chips

Fish and chips is a classic British dish consisting of battered and fried fish served with chunky fries. Often enjoyed with malt vinegar and mushy peas, this comforting meal can be found in traditional pubs and restaurants throughout London.

Afternoon Tea

Afternoon tea is a quintessential British tradition, typically consisting of tea served with finger sandwiches, scones with clotted cream and jam, and an assortment of pastries. It’s a delightful experience that combines elegance with a relaxing tea break.

Full English Breakfast

A full English breakfast is a hearty meal that includes eggs, bacon, sausages, baked beans, black pudding, grilled tomatoes, and toast. It’s a traditional start to the day and a staple of British cuisine, available at cafes and restaurants across London.

London Traditions

Notting Hill Carnival

Held annually in August, the Notting Hill Carnival is one of the largest street festivals in Europe, celebrating Caribbean culture with vibrant costumes, music, and dance. The carnival attracts visitors from around the world and showcases London’s diverse cultural landscape.

Afternoon Tea at The Ritz

Afternoon tea at The Ritz is a luxurious London tradition, offering an opulent setting with exquisite tea, finger sandwiches, scones, and pastries. The Ritz provides a classic British tea experience in an elegant environment, making it a special treat for visitors and locals alike.

Changing of the Guard

The Changing of the Guard is a ceremonial event held at Buckingham Palace, where the Old Guard hands over responsibility to the New Guard. This colorful tradition features marching bands and ceremonial uniforms, offering a glimpse into British royal traditions.
